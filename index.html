The problem

The tools exist. They just weren't built for her.

Every major safety app was designed for Western infrastructure — reliable 4G, stable GPS, responsive emergency services. None of them work offline. None of them were built for a woman in Nairobi catching a 5am matatu because the rent is due.

HerApp's deeper mission

GBV is a societal problem — created by society, and solvable by society.

HerApp doesn't just help women survive dangerous situations — it creates accountability structures that make those situations harder to ignore. When an SOS fires and a contact is notified, that alert is timestamped and logged. When a danger zone is reported by hundreds of women, it cannot be dismissed as an individual complaint. When a police officer is reached through the app, their response — or lack of it — becomes part of a documented record. HerApp holds the right people accountable for the things they are responsible for. Not through anger — through data, community, and an evidence trail that the justice system cannot look away from.

How it works

Four triggers. One result. She's never alone.

HerApp gives a woman four ways to call for help — designed for the moment she is in, not the moment we imagine she might be in.

👆
PRIMARY

2-second hold

Hold the SOS button. A crimson ring fills. Alert fires to her trusted circle in under 500 milliseconds — simultaneously via push and SMS.

🔘
SECONDARY

Volume button sequence

Press volume down 3 times rapidly. SOS fires without touching the screen — for situations where looking at the phone would be dangerous.

🎤
TERTIARY

Panic phrase — opt in

A custom phrase she sets. Detected on-device by Gemma AI — no internet needed, no server required. Screen stays dark. SOS fires silently.

⌚
AUTOMATIC

Journey timeout

She starts a journey. If she doesn't arrive within ETA + 15 minutes and doesn't respond to a check-in, SOS fires automatically on her behalf.

Why we exist

This started with a grandmother catching the early bus.

"I grew up in a household of women. My grandmother and mother were the breadwinners — they carried everything. And carrying everything meant leaving before the sun came up and coming home after it went down. Until the sun was out, I used to lie awake worrying about whether they had gotten to work. I was a little girl in primary school. I felt completely helpless.

My aunt was held at gunpoint near our home. That is not a statistic. That is my family.

I am 19 now, studying at JKUAT. I watch women navigate danger every single day with no tools built for them. HerApp is the answer to a question I have been asking my whole life. I am building it because I know exactly what it feels like to be the person who cannot help — and I refuse to stay that person."

Nicole Owili
Founder, HerApp Technologies · JKUAT, Nairobi
