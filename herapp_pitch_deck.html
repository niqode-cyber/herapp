Why Africa-first is a global advantage
An app that works on 2G in rural Kenya works everywhere. Most Western safety apps don't work without 4G. Offline-first is not a compromise — it is a competitive advantage.
Expansion model
🤝 Partner into new markets
Each city launched with an anchor organisation — a university women's group, NGO, or professional network. We don't enter markets cold. We partner into them.
🌍 City Lead Ambassadors
The most trusted woman in each city's HerApp community becomes the City Lead — coordinating local partnerships and driving growth. Expansion driven by community, not capital.
📍 Expansion sequence
Nairobi → Mombasa → Kisumu → Kampala → Dar es Salaam → Accra. Each city launches only after the previous has a functioning Ambassador community.

Personal connection to the problem — family, community, daily reality
"I grew up in a household of women. My grandmother had to catch the early bus because money was tight — and because money was tight, safety had flown out of the window. Until the sun was out, I used to lie awake worrying about whether they had gotten to work safely. I was a little girl in primary school.

My aunt was held at gunpoint near our home. That is not a statistic. That is my family.

I am building HerApp because I know exactly what it feels like to be the person who cannot help — and I refuse to stay that person."
Nicole Owili
Founder & CEO, HerApp Technologies · Age 19
